Ourika is a beautiful valley located in the Moroccan High Atlas along the Ourika River. It is essentially populated by Berber people speaking Shilha. The valley is surrounded by snow-capped Western High Atlas Mountains and is a fascinating region of Morocco, offering opportunities for mountain hiking and exploration of unique Berber villages.

Tahnaout is a charming town and commune, capital of Al Haouz Province of the Marrakesh-Safi region of Morocco. It is located 34.5 kilometers south of Marrakesh, near the foot of the Atlas Mountains. The town is surrounded by beautiful landscapes and is a great place to experience the rich culture and history of Morocco.

Imlil is a small village in the high Atlas Mountains of Morocco. It is 1,800 meters above sea level and is surrounded by beautiful snow-capped Western High Atlas Mountains. The village is a base for mountain guides and muleteers who work in the area surrounding Jebel Toubkal. Imlil is a fascinating region of Morocco, offering opportunities for mountain hiking and exploration of unique Berber villages.

The Koutoubia Mosque is a beautiful and historic mosque located in the southwest medina quarter of Marrakesh. The mosque’s minaret is the tallest structure in Marrakesh and is visible from miles away. The mosque was founded in 1147 by the Almohad caliph Abd al-Mu’min and is an important example of Almohad architecture and of Moroccan mosque architecture generally.
Jemaa el-Fnaa is a vibrant and colorful square located in the heart of Marrakesh’s medina quarter. It is a must-visit attraction for tourists and locals alike. The square is surrounded by a maze of narrow streets and alleyways that are home to a variety of shops, restaurants, and cafes. A visit to Jemaa el-Fnaa is a great way to experience the rich culture and history of Marrakesh.

The Menara Gardens in Marrakesh are a magical place to visit. The gardens are a popular tourist destination and have been around for centuries. The gardens are surrounded by olive groves and a water reservoir for irrigation purposes. it is really magical.

Riads are traditional Moroccan houses with a central courtyard. They are popular in Marrakesh and offer a unique experience to visitors. Riads are often decorated with intricate tile work, fountains, and lush gardens. They are usually located in the heart of the city, making it easy to explore the local markets and attractions. Riads offer a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of the city and are a great way to experience Moroccan culture.
